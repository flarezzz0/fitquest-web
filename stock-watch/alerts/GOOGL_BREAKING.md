# ⚠️ GOOGL - Breaking: Google Engineer Charged With $1.2M Polymarket Insider Trading

**Date:** May 27-28, 2026
**Severity:** ⚠️ Medium (reputational/legal risk, no direct financial impact)

---

## Headline

Google employee charged with insider trading using Polymarket, profiting $1.2M from confidential "Year in Search" data.

## Summary

A Google staff information security engineer, **Michele Spagnuolo**, was arrested and federally charged with **commodities fraud, wire fraud, and money laundering** for allegedly using confidential internal Google data to place $1.2M in winning bets on Polymarket. He correctly bet that singer **d4vd** would be Google's most searched person in 2025, using non-public "Year in Search" data accessed via internal Google tools.

## Key Details

- **Arrested:** May 27, 2026 in New York
- **Charges:** Commodities fraud, wire fraud, money laundering (Southern District of NY)
- **Platform:** Polymarket (prediction market)
- **Username:** "AlphaRaccoon" — flagged by community in December 2025
- **Profit:** ~$1.2M
- **Status:** Released on $2.25M bond; no plea entered
- **CFTC Action:** Civil case also filed by Commodity Futures Trading Commission

## Google's Response

- Stated employee accessed marketing material via a tool available to all employees
- Called it "a serious breach of our policies"
- Employee placed on leave — "will take the appropriate action"
- Confirmed cooperation with law enforcement
