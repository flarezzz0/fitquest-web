# 🚨 MSFT — Pentagon $9.7B Licensing Deal

**Date:** May 27, 2026 | **Severity:** 🔴 High

## Headline
Pentagon awards Microsoft a record $9.69 billion, 5-year agreement to consolidate enterprise software licenses across the Department of Defense.

## Summary
The Pentagon announced on May 27 a $9.69 billion deal with Microsoft to consolidate Microsoft and other enterprise software licenses across the DoD. The deal aims to cut costs and end license sprawl across the military branch. This is one of the largest government software contracts ever awarded.

## Key Details
- **Value:** $9.69 billion over 5 years
- **Scope:** Consolidation of Microsoft and enterprise software licenses across DoD
- **Source:** Reuters (May 27, 2026)
- **Motivation:** Pentagon aims to cut costs and reduce license fragmentation

## Impact Assessment
- **Bullish:** Massive government deal validates MSFT's enterprise/government dominance; recurring revenue stream locked in for 5 years
- **Azure/Cloud angle:** Likely ties into Microsoft's broader government cloud (Azure Government) ecosystem
- **Competitive:** Beats out potential competition from AWS/Google for government software consolidation
- MSFT has been building on its $22B HoloLens IVAS contract and existing DoD relationships
